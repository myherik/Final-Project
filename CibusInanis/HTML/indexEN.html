Cibus Inanis’ goal is to reduce low degradable waste. We want to start a Zero Waste grocery store which will focus on decreasing waste and decreasing toxic emissions. We want to offer an environmentally friendly and sustainable alternative to consumers.


Less packaging


Vi wish to decrease the amount of unnecessary packing currently being used - especially low degradable packaging like plastic. The goal is to make our store go from using some to using no unnecessary packaging.


In the store we will keep whatever food possible in large containers. This will apply especially to all kinds of dry food – pasta, cereal, nuts, chips etc. The customer will be able to bring their own containers or bags and fill them up, then pay based on weight. Fruit will also not be packaged, but sold separately.


We will sell containers in the store, so the customers can buy them from us directly. This will be especially helpful if they did not bring enough containers from home or if they forgot. The containers will be environmentally friendly and be made from sustainable materials, recyclable plastic or glass. We will also sell reusable shopping bags.


We will not sell disposable plastic bags.


A lot of processed and pre-made food are packaged. Because of this we want to focus on fresh produce, and we would like to have a fresh produce counter.


We understand that, in the current climate, completely eliminating plastic packaging is near impossible. On the products that require some type of packing, we want to focus on the type of packaging. We want to sell products with packaging that is environmentally friendly and made from recycled materials.


Less food waste


Our goal is for each individual person to produce less food waste.


We are not going to have special offers that encourage food waste. An example of this is 3-for-2 offers or price drops, which often lead the customer to buy unnecessary food they do not use and later throw away. Instead, we would like to have personal offers based on the customers spending habits. We will use our app to achieve this.


Locally sourced products


To minimize pollution and emissions that happens before the products arrive at the store, we want to focus on what type of suppliers we use and what type of products we purchase. We will be using as many locally sourced products as possible. This means food will be available on a seasonal basis and not a year-round basis.


We also want to limit emissions by making sure our products have not travelled unnecessarily far. An example of a product travelling unnecessary far is Norwegian salmon that has been sent to China to be packaged.


We will make deals with suppliers to ensure a safe and environmentally friendly delivery, with low emissions and no unnecessary packaging.


Return packaging and old food


We would like to offer our customers a way to return their packaging and food waste. They can deliver these things to us. In return they will be receive points in our app. The points will be turned into offers and discounts they can use in the store.


Our goal is to recycle as much of the packaging as possible.


The food waste will also be recycled. A lot of perfectly good food is thrown away. We wish to make use of this food. We want to produce or own brand which centres on reusability. For example, we want to make banana bread from old bananas. We plan to sell our brand in the store.


The food waste that cannot be reused will be turned into compost and resold in the store or given to farmers.


Membership


We want to create an app for our customers.


The app will offer discounts based on the customer’s shopping habits. This will be a useful tool to prevent food waste.


They will also be able to register points they get from handing in their old food and packaging, which will then be turned into offers and discounts.


Through the app, the customer will be able to see their own progress. The app will give positive encouragements based on how much they have recycled, how much packaging they have saved and how they have helped save the environment. We want to make sure living environmentally friendly is seen as a positive experience for the customer, not a negative one.


Via the app, the customer will be able to unlock different type of memberships based on how often they shop at the store, how much and how much they recycle. With every new level they will receive new offers and discounts. The higher level of membership, the better the discount. This is to give the customer and incentive to use the app, the store and to recycle!

Zero Waste






How is the lifestyle Zero waste?

What is zero waste?


Zero waste is a kind of lifestyle that has become more and more popular with the escalating pressure about the climate problem. The lifestyle is about making as little waste as possible. This is often done by buying as little packaging as possible, trying to cut food waste and being better at reusing stuff and clothes. Zero waste pioneer Bea Johnson has a method to cut down the amount of waste you use, by following the five R’s in this order1:


  • Refuse: refuse the things you don’t need
  • Reduce: reduce the things you do need
  • Reuse: reuse all you can
  • Recycle: recycle the things that can’t be recycled
  • Rot: rot the things that’s left

Why is it important?


In Norway we produce so much waste that if everyone in this world would produce as much waste as us, we would need 3.4 earth globes 2. The problem is that the expenditure increases, and so does the waste. An average Norwegian produces more waste than the average in Europe3. An average European produce around 487 kg of waste, but an average Norwegian produce around 748 kg of waste.


We want to reduce the amount of waste in Norway because waste can have a direct effect on the climate. Bad waste management and littering can change ecosystems, and it has become an increasing problem for the ocean 3. Lots of low degradable waste such as plastic and rubber end up in the ocean, and that creates problems for animals and humans. There is also an indirect consequence of waste, so if the waste doesn’t get recycled, the we lose the resources.


We see this as an opportunity to lower the request for packaging by taking away what is unnecessary. We create so much waste because of packaging of food and products, and so much of this packaging is not necessary. If we don’t need packaging anymore, then we will reduce the amount of waste at home and we will save resources by not producing unnecessary packaging.
